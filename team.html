Brendan Malone

Founding Partner

Brendan previously served as a Policy Manager at Paradigm, where he built the Policy Lab, helped shape the firm's internal strategy and policy research, and advised portfolio founders on policy strategy and engagement. He also led Paradigm's policy efforts across Europe and the UK, as well as the APAC region. He left Paradigm in 2025 to found a company focused on tech and public finance, and has recently consulted with firms including Jump Trading, Plasma, and Patomak Global Partners.

Prior to Paradigm, Brendan worked at the Federal Reserve on financial market infrastructure and payments policy, with a focus on stablecoins. He served as the payments lead for the President's Working Group on Financial Markets Report on Stablecoins and represented the Fed in international standard-setting — authoring multiple BIS CPMI–IOSCO standards documents as the Fed's main international representative. For his stablecoin work, he received the Federal Reserve's Special Achievement Award from Chair Jay Powell in 2021.

Earlier in his career, Brendan managed a large-scale research project for economists at Massachusetts Institute of Technology and Harvard University, including Nobel laureate Josh Angrist, and spent two years based in Warren Buffett's foundation office in Omaha. He earned an M.A. in Applied Economics and a Master of Public Policy from the University of Michigan and a B.A. in Economics from the University of Vermont, where he graduated Phi Beta Kappa.

Jon Fink Isaksen

Founding Partner

Jon Fink Isaksen was most recently Head of International Government Affairs & Strategic Engagement at the Stellar Development Foundation, where he led a team of three, covering policy, government relations and regulatory strategy across the US and key international jurisdictions. Jon engaged at all levels across governments and regulators and key industry stakeholders, advancing DeFi, open permissionless systems and financial access. In addition to global policy activities, Jon worked closely with growth and business teams in assessing priority jurisdictions across the globe.

Before joining Stellar, Jon served as Head of International Policy at Uniswap Labs, where he helped build out the company's international strategy as the first non-US employee. Jon expanded Uniswap Labs' role as a leading voice in DeFi discussions globally, engaging across UK, EU, APAC and the MENA region.

Prior to that, Jon worked for the European Commission (EC) as the head of the digital assets team within the Directorate-General for Financial Stability, Financial Services and Capital Markets Union (DG FISMA). In this role, he led the development and legislative negotiation of the EU's Markets in Crypto-Assets (MiCA) Regulation. Jon also worked on the implementation of the European Commission's Fintech Action plan, coordinating with European Supervisory Authorities as well as the national regulators in the EU on the creation of Innovation Hubs and Regulatory Sandboxes. Lastly, Jon represented the EC in international regulatory forums and expert groups, including the G7, Financial Stability Board (FSB), Basel Committee on Banking Supervision (BCBS), and the Financial Action Task Force (FATF).

Jon started out his career at the Danish Financial Supervisory Authority (DFSA), where he first worked on IT transformation projects, then payments, fintech and digital asset policy. Jon helped set up the DFSA's Innovation Hub and Regulatory Sandbox.

Reid M. Yager

Founding Partner

Reid M. Yager most recently served as Head of Policy and Strategic Communications at Flashbots, where he engaged global regulators on DeFi market structure and led the development of industry principles on base layer neutrality and MEV. He has advised FINRA, SEC, ESMA, European Commission, and U.S. Congress on crypto regulatory topics. He co-authored "Leveraging Blockchain in a New Era of Antitrust," published in the Stanford Journal of Blockchain Law & Policy, arguing that blockchain offers practical tools for promoting competitive markets and modernizing antitrust enforcement.

Before Flashbots, Reid served as Global Director of Strategic Communications at Tezos. He led awareness and education campaigns for the network, positioned Tezos as a cultural center for generative art, and represented the organization at the Venice Biennale and Art Basel, where he spoke on the accessibility of the art market and the barriers facing artists in emerging markets.

Earlier, he managed advocacy and public affairs at FS Vector in Washington, D.C., representing clients including Meta's Libra Project, the Proof of Stake Alliance, and Ripple. He entered the industry in 2017 as an early employee at Wachsman, a blockchain communications consultancy, rising to Chief of Staff. He began his career in politics, working on Hillary for America and serving as a researcher at the Democratic National Committee.
